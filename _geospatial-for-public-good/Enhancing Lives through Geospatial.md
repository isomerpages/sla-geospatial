<p>In Singapore's compact landscape, geospatial information and technology
are indispensable for optimising public services and fostering a "Smart
Nation." Within the social sector, the OneMap platform provides tools and
functions for the visualisation and analysis of demographic data, helping
social service agencies identify areas and communities with specific needs.
Initiatives like the barrier-free access routing function on OneMap directly
support the Enabling Master Plan 2030, enhancing inclusivity and enabling
better resource allocation for a more equitable society.</p>
<p></p>
<p>For the healthcare sector, geospatial technology underpins proactive and
reactive strategies. The newly launched Singapore Geospatial Master Plan
(2024-2033) explicitly targets preventive healthcare, exemplified by collaborations
between SLA and the Ministry of Health Office for Healthcare Transformation
(MOHT). Projects like the Digital Local Connect (DLC) piloted in Yio Chu
Kang, leverage geospatial data to help grassroots leaders and volunteers
guide residents to nearby healthcare providers, community programmes, aligning
with the HealthierSG initiative. Furthermore, mapping disease clusters
and healthcare infrastructure across the island enables more efficient
emergency response and strategic planning for future healthcare needs in
Singapore's dense urban environment.</p>
